Worm Barrel

A worm farm sounds like a sophisticated piece of agricultural land for the high intensity growth of worms. I have on several occasions over the past few years sketched out the “perfect” farm for my future worms. Each time I find the ideas becoming more complex and detailed as I learn what is actually required to get worms going. The only reason it has taken me this long to put together a semidecent worm farm is because of the projected costs involved with my sketches and the lack of direct application.

I am now in a position where I require worm tea to add to my tool kit of moving beyond organic on the farm. This, along with a year of collecting potentially useful things has finally made me make the farm. The design I went with is nothing like I have sketched over the years but rather a combination of the parts I had available. Instead of carefully sketching the perfect farm and measuring each piece of wood to the correct dimensions I have opted with the build upwards approach. I lay out the various components that I have to build the farm, studied them for a few minutes while I imagined how they could be put together. This approach, although not always visually elegant, has proved useful in the past and is the best way to stop thinking and start doing.

A quick digress:

Is thinking doing? Does thinking prelude doing? If one thinks more does he do more? Imagine on the average day you have 10 ideas that can be done. If you on average act on 1/10 thoughts then you would be doing 1 thing a day. Now, if you think more do you come up with more ideas that can be done? If instead of 10 ideas you have 100 ideas in a day and act on 1/10 then you would be doing 10 things a day. There is surely a point at which the two lines path and thinking begins to negatively impact doing. This will be a personal barrier that we each need to find. Think just enough, but no less!
