Fresh Rots

A collection of my writing, 2010–2019 — the land, science, and building things by hand.

  • 2010-06-30Zero to Hero: a change of perspective

    In 2010 I wrote a popular article for The Fishing and Hunting Journal as part of our honours course at Rhodes University. I chose to write about the sea barbel and its reproductive strategy. Quite an amazing little fish whom I have the utmost respect for.

  • 2012-09-18My PhD Project Conference

    In the early stages of starting a PhD I attended a NRF conference which prepares candidates for the journey. I tried to summarize the key message I took from the experience in a post I now share with you.

  • 2017-09-08Worm Barrel

    The perfect worm farm is a matter of perspective. Having a worm farm is the first step in getting there. After several years of doodling this project I have finally got a working prototype.

  • 2017-09-11Ram pump project 2016

    In 2016 we decided to build a ram pump, a small project that got really technical and taught us how hard it is to replicate a design without any technical data.

  • 2017-09-15YouTube - Mac a Ram Pump in the Forest

    Last year I set out to build a ram pump on my farm, and this is a short video of my current working version, covering the critical details I found other tutorials left out.

  • 2017-09-28Backyard biochar

    Biochar is a form of carbon produced through the incomplete combustion of carbon-based feedstock by pyrolysis, and this post covers its soil-amendment characteristics and how to make it in your backyard using a double barrel retort.

  • 2019-04-30Macadamia madness

    On Easter Monday (22 April 2019) KwaZulu Natal and the Eastern Cape had torrential rain; I recorded 474 mm in 48 hours and went out to photograph the soil erosion across a large macadamia development.
